New to coding? Mind your "Ps"!

Hi, my name is Anna and my advice is focused on what I call the "5 Ps" of coding.

Patience

Be kind to yourself. When things become challenging, and they will, take a deep breath, stay calm, take a break and know things will all workout in the end.

Planning

One of the best things you can do to reduce frustration is to plan. Plan out your projects, your next step and your ideas. Coding without a plan is like trying to write with blinders on. You'll soon find you're all over the place.

Problem Solving

Having good problem solving skills is a great asset. Here are the steps I use: Identif the problem. Gather information about the root cause of the problem. Generate possible solutions. Evaluate ideas and then try them out until you find one that works. Evaluate.

Practice

Practice! Practice! Practice! The old saying "Practice makes perfect!" is true. Daily practice truly is the key to reinforce what you are learning.

Perserverance

Whatever you do, don't give up. Every step you take is a step closer to your goal. And if you follow the second P, Planning, you will find that you'll complete your steps in record time.


You're closer to your goal than you think. Good luck!
